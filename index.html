01
Product Design Electronics Raspberry Pi

NutriSync

An automated system to manage nutrient and water levels for indoor hydroponic gardens — eliminating the need to manually measure and refill reservoirs to keep plants healthy.

SF State University 2026

My design uses a Raspberry Pi to process data from electrical conductivity and water sensors, which then triggers pumps to add nutrients and water until a target value is reached.

One of the primary challenges was the physical packaging of the device. The custom housing I designed had to be compact enough for a tabletop while housing a Raspberry Pi, pumps, relays, cables, and two liquid reservoirs. Balancing this small footprint with the need to fit all components — while ensuring the product was visually appealing and user-friendly — required several iterations in CAD and rounds of user testing to ensure the system was both functional and maintainable.

Engineering Manufacturing Capstone

Carbon Fiber Mountain Bike

Designed and built a full-suspension mountain bike using recycled wood and carbon fiber composite for Celilo Cycles — a six-month capstone project that earned an award from Daimler Trucks North America at the OSU 2022 Engineering Exposition.

Oregon State University 2022

My role included researching and determining the frame geometry, creating a cardboard prototype, and building the initial wooden prototype. I also contributed to refining the second wooden prototype, 3D-printing small components for accurate sizing, and assisting with project reports.

04
Engineering Arduino Robotics

Rhythm Robot

A sound-reactive dancing robot built at OSU that responds to music in real time — using FFT beat detection, servo motors, and dynamic LED patterns to translate amplitude and frequency into movement and light.

Oregon State University 2021

The robot used a SparkFun Sound Detector to measure sound amplitude and frequency, triggering changes in LED colors, flashing patterns, and dance movements via four servo motors. I implemented a fast Fourier transform (FFT) function using EasyFFT to filter noise, detect beats, and identify common frequencies.

I 3D-printed the robot's body, set up the sound sensor, wrote all FFT and beat detection code, and designed a support system for the breadboard and Arduino to allow controlled movement.

Product Design Consumer Goods 3D Modeling

Lumaire Nightstand

A nightstand designed for the busy parent — touch-controlled LED lighting, motion-activated floor lights, soft-close drawers, and child-safe rounded edges all integrated into a single, organized form.

SF State University 2024

The design aimed to address common challenges like clutter, harsh lighting, and safety concerns while providing functional and user-friendly features. Touch-controlled LED lights allow adjustable brightness, making it easy to operate without disturbing sleep.

The motion-activated floor lights provide illumination for nighttime movement, reducing the risk of accidents. Smooth, rounded edges ensure safety for children, while soft-close drawers prevent noise during use.

06
Engineering Internship SLA Prototyping

Rock Tumbler Mount

Designed the housing and motor mount for a tumbler assembly at Blue Marble — ensuring compliance with strict guidelines and producing a functional SLA 3D-printed prototype that validated the design.

Blue Marble — Internship 2022

I designed the housing and motor mount to meet specific dimensional guidelines provided by Blue Marble, ensuring compatibility with the existing tumbler assembly. The design went through several CAD iterations before being validated with a stereolithography (SLA) 3D print.

The SLA prototype confirmed fit, clearance, and structural adequacy — giving the team confidence in the design ahead of tooling. This internship project strengthened my skills in design for manufacturing constraints and rapid prototyping workflows.

Product Design Consumer Electronics

CloudAir One

A combination air purifier and dehumidifier with a whimsical cloud form — a transparent water container lets users watch the dehumidification process as droplets collect and fall.

SF State University 2024

Air is drawn in through the top, purified through a HEPA filter, then dehumidified and released through the base of the cloud. The top cloud section fits easily into the base, making it simple to remove and empty the water.

08
Product Design Educational Toy Team Project

Create-A-Plant

An educational toy designed for children during COVID-19 — 3D-printed interlocking plant parts that teach botany through hands-on assembly, built for under $25 and produced for $12.64.

Oregon State University 2020

Create-A-Plant consists of 3D-printed plant parts that children can assemble, with leaves and flowers that interlock with stems and a pot base. Each part is labeled with its botanical name to help children learn about plants.

I contributed to all stages — researching SET factors, interviewing two families, defining engineering specifications, sketching concepts, modeling the pot and stems, and producing the final PLA/ABS prototype.
